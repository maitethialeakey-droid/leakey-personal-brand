Fair Value Gap Strategy: Complete Trading Guide

Posted on April 13, 2026 | 13 min read | By Leakey Maitethia

What is a Fair Value Gap (FVG)?

A Fair Value Gap (FVG) is a price imbalance created when the market gaps over a level without trading there. When price moves rapidly (usually on high volume), it can skip over a price level entirely. This creates an "unfair" area that the market naturally returns to fill.

Fair Value Gaps are one of the most reliable setups in ICT trading because they represent areas of institutional interest.

How Fair Value Gaps Form

The Three-Candle Pattern

A Fair Value Gap is identified by a specific three-candle pattern:

  1. Candle 1: Normal candle with a body and wicks
  2. Candle 2: Large gap candle that gaps away from Candle 1
  3. Candle 3: Candle that doesn't fill the gap

Example: Bullish FVG

Imagine these price levels:

  • Candle 1: High 5,100, Low 5,050
  • Candle 2: High 5,200, Low 5,150 (gaps up from Candle 1)
  • Candle 3: High 5,180, Low 5,140 (doesn't fill the gap)

The gap between Candle 1's high (5,100) and Candle 2's low (5,150) is the Fair Value Gap. This area is "unfair" and the market will likely return to fill it.

Types of Fair Value Gaps

Bullish FVG (Imbalance Up)

Occurs when price gaps up rapidly. The gap between the high of the first candle and the low of the second candle is the FVG. Price typically returns to fill this gap, creating a buying opportunity.

Bearish FVG (Imbalance Down)

Occurs when price gaps down rapidly. The gap between the low of the first candle and the high of the second candle is the FVG. Price typically returns to fill this gap, creating a selling opportunity.

Trading Fair Value Gaps

Strategy 1: FVG Retest Trade

The most common FVG strategy is to wait for price to return and retest the gap:

  1. Identify the FVG
  2. Wait for price to return to the FVG area
  3. Enter when price retests the FVG
  4. Stop loss: Just beyond the FVG
  5. Target: Next resistance/support level

Strategy 2: FVG Breakout Trade

Sometimes price doesn't return to fill the FVG immediately. Instead, it breaks through it:

  1. Identify the FVG
  2. Wait for price to break through the FVG
  3. Enter on the breakout
  4. Stop loss: Below the FVG
  5. Target: Next major level

Strategy 3: FVG Confluence Trade

The most powerful trades combine FVG with other signals:

  • FVG + Market Structure Break
  • FVG + SMT Divergence
  • FVG + Support/Resistance Level
  • FVG + Volume Spike

Identifying Quality FVGs

High-Quality FVG Characteristics

  • Large gap: The bigger the gap, the more significant the imbalance
  • High volume: High volume on the gap candle indicates institutional activity
  • At key levels: FVGs at support/resistance are more reliable
  • In trending markets: FVGs work better in strong trends
  • Multiple timeframe confirmation: FVGs on multiple timeframes are stronger

Low-Quality FVG Characteristics

  • Small gap (less than 0.5% of price)
  • Low volume on the gap candle
  • In the middle of a consolidation
  • During low-volatility periods

Common FVG Mistakes

  1. Trading every FVG: Only trade high-quality FVGs with confluence
  2. Ignoring volume: Low volume FVGs often fail
  3. Poor stop placement: Place stops just beyond the FVG
  4. Overleveraging: Use proper position sizing
  5. Trading against the trend: Trade FVGs in the direction of the trend

FVG on Different Timeframes

Fair Value Gaps work on all timeframes:

  • Daily: Large FVGs represent major imbalances
  • 4-Hour: Good for intermediate trades
  • 1-Hour: Good for day trading
  • 15-Minute: Good for scalping

Higher timeframe FVGs are more reliable than lower timeframe FVGs.

Advanced: Multiple FVGs

Sometimes multiple FVGs form in sequence. When this happens:

  • The first FVG is usually filled quickly
  • The second FVG becomes the primary target
  • Multiple FVGs indicate strong institutional activity

Conclusion

Fair Value Gaps are one of the most reliable setups in price action trading. By identifying quality FVGs, waiting for confirmation, and combining them with other signals, you can build a high-probability trading system.

Remember: The best FVG trades are those that combine multiple confluence factors. Don't trade every FVG—only trade the best ones.
