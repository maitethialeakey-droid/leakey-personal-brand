Liquidity Concepts: How Smart Money Hunts Stops

Posted on April 11, 2026 | 12 min read | By Leakey Maitethia

What is Liquidity in Trading?

Liquidity refers to the ease with which an asset can be bought or sold. In the context of ICT trading, liquidity also refers to the stop losses of retail traders. Institutional traders deliberately move price to collect this liquidity before reversing in their intended direction.

Understanding liquidity is crucial because it explains why price often moves against you before moving in your favor.

Liquidity Pools Explained

Where Do Retail Traders Place Stops?

Retail traders tend to place stops at obvious technical levels:

  • Just below support levels
  • Just above resistance levels
  • Just beyond swing highs/lows
  • At round numbers (like $100, $50, etc.)

Liquidity Pools Form at These Levels

When many retail traders place stops at the same level, a "liquidity pool" forms. Institutional traders know where these stops are and deliberately move price to collect them.

How Smart Money Hunts Liquidity

The Liquidity Hunt Pattern

Smart money follows this pattern:

  1. Identify liquidity: Find where retail traders have placed stops
  2. Move price to collect: Push price to trigger the stops
  3. Reverse direction: Once stops are collected, move price in the intended direction
  4. Profit: Execute their main position

Example: Bullish Liquidity Hunt

Imagine a stock at $100:

  • Support is at $95
  • Retail traders place stops at $94 (just below support)
  • Smart money pushes price down to $94, triggering all the stops
  • Once stops are collected, smart money buys aggressively
  • Price reverses and goes up to $110

Retail traders get stopped out at $94. Smart money profits from $94 to $110.

Identifying Liquidity Levels

Key Liquidity Levels

Liquidity pools form at these key levels:

  • Swing highs: Where buyers stop buying
  • Swing lows: Where sellers stop selling
  • Support/Resistance: Where many traders have orders
  • Round numbers: Psychological levels like $100, $50, etc.
  • Previous highs/lows: Where many traders have stops

Mitigated vs Unmitigated Liquidity

Mitigated Liquidity

When price touches a liquidity level and collects the stops, that liquidity is "mitigated." Once mitigated, price often reverses sharply.

Unmitigated Liquidity

When price approaches a liquidity level but doesn't touch it, that liquidity remains "unmitigated." Price often returns to collect this unmitigated liquidity later.

Trading Liquidity Hunts

Setup 1: Liquidity Grab and Reversal

  1. Identify a key liquidity level (swing high or low)
  2. Wait for price to approach the level
  3. Watch for price to touch the level (collecting liquidity)
  4. Enter when price reverses from the level
  5. Stop loss: Beyond the liquidity level
  6. Target: Next liquidity level

Setup 2: Liquidity Sweep

Sometimes smart money sweeps multiple liquidity levels in sequence:

  1. Price breaks below a swing low (collecting liquidity)
  2. Price then breaks below another swing low (collecting more liquidity)
  3. After collecting all liquidity, price reverses sharply
  4. Enter on the reversal

Combining Liquidity with Other Signals

Liquidity + Market Structure

When price breaks market structure AND collects liquidity, it's a strong signal that smart money is accumulating.

Liquidity + Fair Value Gap

When a liquidity hunt occurs near a Fair Value Gap, the probability increases significantly.

Liquidity + Volume

High volume on a liquidity hunt indicates institutional activity. Low volume hunts often fail.

Common Liquidity Mistakes

  1. Placing stops at obvious levels: Smart money targets these stops
  2. Placing stops at round numbers: Too many traders use these levels
  3. Not understanding liquidity: Thinking price movement is random
  4. Trading against liquidity hunts: Don't fight smart money
  5. Ignoring volume: Low volume hunts often fail

Protecting Yourself from Liquidity Hunts

Stop Loss Placement

Place your stops at logical levels, not at obvious round numbers:

  • Place stops just beyond swing highs/lows, not at them
  • Avoid round numbers like $100, $50, etc.
  • Use wider stops to avoid being hunted

Position Sizing

If you're worried about being hunted, reduce your position size and use wider stops.

Confluence Trading

Only trade setups with multiple confluence factors. This reduces the chance of being stopped out by a liquidity hunt.

Advanced: Predicting Liquidity Hunts

Experienced traders can predict where smart money will hunt liquidity:

  • Look at where retail traders are likely to place stops
  • Identify key support/resistance levels
  • Watch for price approaching these levels
  • Predict the direction of the hunt based on market structure

Conclusion

Smart money hunts liquidity to collect retail stop losses. By understanding this concept, you can:

  • Avoid placing stops at obvious levels
  • Trade liquidity hunts for profit
  • Understand why price often moves against you before moving in your favor
  • Follow smart money instead of fighting them
